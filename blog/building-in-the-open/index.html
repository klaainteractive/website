Why we build in the open

February 1, 2025 · 2 min read

There’s a temptation in the technology industry to operate in secrecy. To hoard information, protect roadmaps, and reveal things only when they’re polished and ready for applause.

We’ve chosen a different path.

Transparency as discipline

Building in the open isn’t about marketing. It’s about holding ourselves to a higher standard. When you share your work publicly — your decisions, your reasoning, your mistakes — you create accountability that no internal process can match.

What this means in practice

We share our thinking. Not just the conclusions, but the process. The tradeoffs we considered, the options we rejected, and why.

We acknowledge what we don’t know. Pretending to have all the answers isn’t confidence — it’s fragility. We’re comfortable saying “we’re still figuring this out.”

We welcome scrutiny. If our reasoning is flawed, we want to know. The goal isn’t to be right — it’s to get it right.

The boundaries

Transparency doesn’t mean broadcasting everything. We protect our team’s privacy, our users’ data, and competitive advantages where they genuinely matter. But our default is openness, and we require a specific reason to deviate from it.
